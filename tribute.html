Tribute to Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

"Give me blood and
I will give you freedom!"

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was a prominent Indian nationalist leader who attempted to rid India of British rule with the help of Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan during World War II. He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Orissa, India.

Netaji formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) to overthrow British rule in India and sought the help of Axis powers to do so. His famous slogan, "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" stirred the masses.

Though his ultimate fate remains a subject of controversy, his contribution to the Indian independence movement remains significant and he continues to inspire generations of Indians.

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose died under mysterious circumstances in 1945, but his legacy lives on.
